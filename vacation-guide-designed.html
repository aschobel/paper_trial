"Nonstop flights, perfect weather, and the best food city on earth"

What you'd do in a week

  • ⛩️ Meiji Shrine + Harajuku — serene forest → Takeshita Street chaos
  • 🏔️ Day trip: Hakone — hot springs ryokan, Mt. Fuji views, open-air museum

Why it wins: Only destination with nonstop both ways. ZIPAIR is JAL's low-cost carrier — basic but comfortable. $767 out is the cheapest transpacific fare on the board. Post-sakura Tokyo is warm, uncrowded, and green.

"The cheapest trip that also happens to be one of the best"

OUT · SFO → OAX · Apr 17 American · 10h · 1 stop (DFW)

What you'd do in a week

  • 🍫 Mole tasting — 7 varieties of mole, each from a different region. Nothing like it anywhere.
  • 🥃 Mezcal trail — drive to Matatlán ("world capital of mezcal"), visit palenques, taste artisanal spirits
  • 🏛️ Monte Albán — Zapotec ruins on a mountaintop, 2,500 years old, UNESCO site
  • 🎨 Textile villages — Teotitlán del Valle (hand-woven rugs), natural dye workshops
  • 🌮 Street food tour — tlayudas, chapulines (grasshoppers!), tamales, memelas
  • 🏖️ Day trip: Puerto Escondido — Pacific surf beach, 6h drive or 30min flight
  • 🌳 Hierve el Agua — petrified waterfalls, natural infinity pools overlooking the valley
  • 🛍️ Mercado Benito Juárez — chocolate, cheese, chapulines, crafts
  • 👨‍🍳 Cooking class — learn mole negro (30+ ingredients, 3 days to make traditionally)
  • 🌅 Rooftop mezcal bars — Los Danzantes, Mezcaloteca (reservation-only tasting room)
Alice factor: Cooking classes (mole negro from scratch), artisan textile markets, mezcal distillery tours in Matatlán valley, gorgeous rooftop restaurants.
Why it's special: Total trip cost (flights + hotels + food + activities) under $3,000 for two. Oaxacan cuisine is arguably Mexico's greatest — and Mexico's cuisine is UNESCO-recognized. This is a food and culture trip, not a beach trip.

Why it's special: The ultimate "nobody's been there yet" destination. Georgia is having its moment in the natural wine world. Absurdly cheap once you arrive. The food is a revelation — think dumplings, cheese bread, and wine from clay vessels buried underground.

Also Considered

Interesting destinations that didn't make the cut for this particular trip

Sicily

$2,573 flights (2 pax) · 66h total travel · 1 stop, long layover · 68°F ☀️

Beautiful island with great food and history, but the 36h outbound and 30h return with long layovers make it impractical for a 7-day trip. Better suited for a longer Mediterranean itinerary.

Marrakech

$2,872 flights (2 pax) · 68h total travel · 1 stop, long layover · 81°F ☀️

Incredible sensory overload — souks, riads, Atlas Mountains — but 36h outbound and 32h return with long layovers eat into the trip. The highest total travel time on the board after Cape Town makes it tough for one week.

Cape Town

$3,769 flights (2 pax) · 60h total travel · 1 stop each · 76°F ☀️

One of the world's great cities — Table Mountain, Stellenbosch wine, penguins — but expensive flights and 60h of total travel time make it a better candidate for a 10+ day trip. Worth revisiting when longer travel is an option.

Penny's Take

If you love directs

Tokyo wins, full stop. Nonstop ZIPAIR both ways, 20h total, cheapest flight after Oaxaca. Post-sakura spring Tokyo is warm, green, and uncrowded. You'll eat the best food of your lives.

If Alice's wine heart wins

Tbilisi is the dark horse. Birthplace of wine, $40/day, and a story nobody else has. Or Croatia, where everything is already researched and ready to book.

Best bang for buck

Oaxaca. $1,287 flights, $50/day on the ground. Under $3K total for the trip. The food alone is worth it.

The "what if" pick

Lisbon on nonstop TAP. Europe with a direct flight, natural wine, pastéis de nata, day trip to Porto. $2,861 all-in nonstop.
